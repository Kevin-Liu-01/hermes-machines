# Content Strategy

## Positioning Arc

The progression: "talented builder" (early) -> "dangerous early engineer with taste" (mid) -> "operator mode" (mature). Each phase has specific content types and proof requirements.

## Content Calendar

**Phase 1 (Builder)**: Dev diary + frontend theses (architecture posts, custom renderers, design system work)

**Phase 2 (Taste)**: Design system work (behavior vs aesthetic control, component library positioning)

**Phase 3 (Operator)**: PMF, agent revenue, conversion, growth loops

## Weekly Rhythm

- 5 X posts (3 build logs, 1 take, 1 trend reaction)
- 2 LinkedIn posts (narrative, credibility-building)
- 15-20 meaningful replies across both platforms
- 5 DMs to people whose work connects
- 1 artifact/diagram per week (screenshot, architecture sketch, code snippet)

## Four Content Pillars

**Build in Public** -- prove craft. Dev diaries, custom renderers, design systems, frontend patterns. Reaction: "this person ships ambitious technical work."

**Field Notes** -- prove applied relevance. SEO/GEO, agent demos, event learnings, growth loops. Reaction: "this person is close to real product usage."

**Product and Market Diagnosis** -- prove thinking range. PMF, crowded-market strategy, conversion. Reaction: "this person has an original point of view."

**Human Context** -- sparingly. Hackathon summaries, founder conversations, travel moments with real story. Reaction: "this person is in motion."

## Content Mix (per 10 posts)

| Bucket | Share |
|--------|-------|
| Technical build logs | 5 |
| Product and market lessons | 3 |
| Field notes and human context | 2 |

## Quality Gate

1. Could only you post this exact thing?
2. Is there a concrete proof element?
3. Does it teach, reveal, or frame something?
4. Is the first line strong enough without baiting?
5. Is the close inviting without begging?
6. Does it reinforce the target reputation?

If a draft fails checks 1 or 2, kill it or rewrite it.

## Capture Schema

For each candidate idea: working title, pillar, one-sentence claim, specific proof, why it matters now, best platform/format, artifact to attach, confidentiality check. If claim, proof, or "why it matters" is missing, the post is not ready.

## Channel Roles

**X**: speed, sharp takes, build fragments, event reactions, technical snapshots. Should feel alive and slightly experimental.

**LinkedIn**: credibility, narrative posts, milestone reflections, market synthesis. Should feel sharper, calmer, more legible to operators.
